National Capital Region

"Bharat Ki Rajdhani"

The National Capital Region (NCR) in India is a sprawling metropolitan area encompassing Delhi and its surrounding urban regions in Haryana, Uttar Pradesh,
and Rajasthan. It is a major economic and political hub, housing government institutions, corporate headquarters, and cultural landmarks. The NCR's development
is governed by the National Capital Region Planning Board (NCRPB), focusing on sustainable growth and infrastructure development across its constituent areas..

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India. It commemorates the Indian soldiers who died during World War I and the Afghan Wars. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it stands at a height of 42 meters and is surrounded by lush lawns, making it a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of national pride.

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a Bahá'í House of Worship known for its distinctive lotus flower-like architecture. It is open to people of all religions and serves as a place for meditation, prayer, and reflection. The temple is surrounded by lush gardens and is a prominent symbol of unity and peace.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi, India. It is a 73-meter tall minaret made of red sandstone and marble, built in the early 13th century. The minaret is a symbol of Indo-Islamic architecture and is surrounded by several other historical structures and ruins, making it a significant tourist attraction in Delhi.

Sansad Bhavan (old parliament)

Sansad Bhavan, also known as the Parliament House, is the seat of the Parliament of India located in New Delhi. It was designed by British architect Edwin Lutyens and Herbert Baker and inaugurated in 1927. The building houses the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States) chambers along with other important parliamentary offices and facilities.

Humanyun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb is a magnificent mausoleum located in Delhi, India. It was built in the 16th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The tomb's impressive Mughal architecture, including its large dome and symmetrical gardens, makes it a popular historical attraction.

Swaminaraym Akshardham Temple

Akshardham is a Hindu temple complex in Delhi, India, dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan. It showcases traditional Indian culture, spirituality, and architecture through exhibitions, gardens, and a breathtaking temple. The complex is known for its intricately carved stone work, beautiful sculptures, and a peaceful atmosphere for visitors to explore and learn about Hindu heritage.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a significant historical monument. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century and served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors. The fort's architecture reflects a blend of Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles, making it a remarkable symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

Chandni Chowk

Chandni Chowk is one of the oldest and busiest markets in Old Delhi, India, known for its vibrant atmosphere, narrow lanes, and diverse range of goods. It's famous for its street food, including parathas, chaat, and jalebis, and is a hub for shopping traditional Indian attire, jewelry, and spices. The area also boasts historical landmarks like the Red Fort, Jama Masjid, and the Gurdwara Sis Ganj Sahib, adding to its cultural significance.

Delhi Jama Masjid

This great mosque of Old Delhi is the largest in India, with a courtyard capable of holding 25,000 devotees. It was begun in 1644 and ended up being the final architectural extravagance of Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor who built the Taj Mahal and the Red Fort.
